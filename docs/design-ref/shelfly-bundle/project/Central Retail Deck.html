02  ·  Shifts
Confidential — Internal06 / 14
Consumer shifts

Three forces are redrawing the map for Thai retail.

Each shift has been visible for years. What's new is that all three are now structurally material to revenue — and each points to a different investment priority.

Premiumisation Health & wellness Tourism rebound
01

The premium middle is widening.

Imported-food basket up 19%. Luxury fashion durable. Value still anchors — but the middle is trading up, not down, for the first time since 2019.

02

Wellness is a department, not an aisle.

Tops Care, pharmacy formats, and Supersports are converging. The customer who buys running shoes now expects supplements, clinics, and classes under the same roof.

03

Inbound tourism has returned — differently.

Chinese and Indian visitors are back above 2019 levels. They shop more narrowly and more digitally. Our tax-refund flow needs to match that behaviour.

03  ·  Then & now
Baseline FY22 compared to target FY2610 / 14
Then and now

What four years of omnichannel work has changed.

FY22 · baseline

Before
  • Channel P&Ls run separately — digital reports as a cost centre.
  • Customer identity lives per banner; cross-shopping invisible.
  • Fulfilment from store backrooms; 48-hour SLA at best.
  • Associates paid on store walk-ins only.
  • Europe treated as a financial holding — no operational link.

FY26 · target

Now
  • One P&L per customer journey — digital is a channel, not a silo.
  • Unified identity across nine banners; 19M linked households.
  • Seven hub DCs with 2-hour Bangkok, same-day metro coverage.
  • Associate compensation follows the customer, in-store or online.
  • Shared buying, loyalty, and data across Bangkok, Milan, Berlin, London.
